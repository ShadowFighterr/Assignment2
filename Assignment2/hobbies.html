Reading Books

I like reading books that are really useful in my life. Rather than novellas and other literature, I prefer to read informative books. Personally, I would recommend the following books:

  1. Lay, D.C. (2016). Linear Algebra and Its Applications
    • This textbook introduces fundamental concepts of linear algebra, emphasizing practical applications. It is well-regarded for its clear explanations, examples, and exercises.
  2. Cormen, T.H., et al. (2009). Introduction to Algorithms (3rd ed.)
    • This book is a comprehensive introduction to computer algorithms, making their design and analysis accessible to all readers.
  3. Laaksonen, A. (2018). Competitive Programmer's Handbook
    • A practical guide covering essential algorithms and problem-solving techniques for competitive programming.
